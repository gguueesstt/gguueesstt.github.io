The pattern organ is a camera-based digital musical instrument, that uses a relatively direct mapping between camera data and audio wavetable. The simplicity of these mappings enables the audification of camera data in a way that responds closely to material behaviours playing out in front of the lens. 

![Project One Screenshot](/imgs/Pattorgan2025.png)

More information on the pattern organ and its design can be found in these research papers: 
- [Entangling with Light and Shadow](https://nime.org/proc/nime2025_7/index.html), NIME'25, Utrecht, Netherlands
- [Harmonic Materials](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3771594.3771606), AM.ICAD'25, Braga, Portugal

![Screenshot of PD Patches](/imgs/PD_PATCH.png)

The Pure Data patch (which is open source), can be found on [github](https://github.com/gguueesstt/pattern-organ)

The pattern organ has been used in a number of workshops and performances. Below is a video from a performance at Margin Forever, Cube Cinema, 2025.
